<p><p>An Optical Add-Drop Multiplexer (OADM) is a device used in optical networks to add or drop specific wavelengths from a fiber optic transmission without disrupting the entire signal. The market is divided into two main types: Fixed OADMs, which offer dedicated channels for specific wavelengths, providing reliable but inflexible service; and Reconfigurable OADMs, which allow dynamic allocation of channels, enabling more flexible traffic management and efficient bandwidth utilization. Both serve crucial roles in enhancing network performance.</p></p>
