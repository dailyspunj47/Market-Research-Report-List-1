<p><p>Knee arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat various knee joint disorders. It involves the insertion of a small camera and instruments through tiny incisions around the knee, allowing surgeons to visualize and operate on the internal structures of the joint without the need for large incisions. This technique is commonly performed for conditions such as meniscus tears, ligament injuries, and cartilage damage.</p><p>The knee arthroscopy market is experiencing significant growth, driven by an increasing prevalence of knee injuries, a rising elderly population, and advancements in arthroscopic technologies. The shift towards minimally invasive procedures has made knee arthroscopy a preferred choice among both patients and healthcare providers, contributing to its popularity. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.40% during the forecast period, reflecting strong demand for innovative surgical instruments and techniques.</p><p>Additionally, emerging trends include the integration of robotics and artificial intelligence in surgical procedures, as well as a focus on developing biodegradable implants.
