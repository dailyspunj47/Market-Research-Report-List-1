<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are advanced tools used in mechanobiology to manipulate microscopic biological samples. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and move particles, allowing researchers to study cellular mechanics at high precision. In contrast, magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to exert forces on magnetically-labeled biomolecules, facilitating the exploration of molecular dynamics and interaction forces. Both technologies enable detailed analysis of biological processes, contributing to the understanding of cell behavior and biomolecular functions.</p></p>
