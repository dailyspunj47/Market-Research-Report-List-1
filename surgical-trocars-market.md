<p><p>The surgical trocars market is divided into two main types: reusable trocars and disposable trocars. Reusable trocars are designed for multiple uses, made from durable materials, and require sterilization after each procedure, making them cost-effective for facilities with high surgical volumes. In contrast, disposable trocars are single-use devices that offer convenience and reduced infection risk, as they do not require sterilization. The choice between these types often depends on surgical preferences, cost considerations, and infection control protocols.</p></p>
