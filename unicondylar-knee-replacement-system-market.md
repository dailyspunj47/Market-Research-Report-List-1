<p><p>The Unicondylar Knee Replacement System market is primarily classified into two types based on fixation methods: Cemented and Uncemented Fixation Types. Cemented fixation involves using bone cement to securely attach the implant to the existing bone, providing immediate stability and quick weight-bearing. In contrast, uncemented fixation relies on porous materials that encourage bone ingrowth over time, promoting natural biological fixation. Both types cater to different patient needs and surgeon preferences, impacting recovery times and long-term outcomes.</p></p>
