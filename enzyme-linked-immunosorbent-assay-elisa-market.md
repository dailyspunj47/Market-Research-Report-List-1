<p><p>Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) is a popular laboratory technique used to detect and quantify proteins, hormones, and antibodies in various samples. The market encompasses several types: Sandwich ELISA is used for detecting large molecules through a "sandwich" of antibodies; Indirect ELISA measures antibodies by using a secondary antibody for signal amplification; Competitive ELISA assesses the concentration of a target by competition with a labeled antigen. Other types include double-antibody sandwich ELISA and direct ELISA, catering to diverse research needs.</p></p>
