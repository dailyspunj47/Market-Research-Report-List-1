<p><ul><li>Multistage Flash Distillation</li><li>Multi-Effect Distillation</li><li>Vapor Compression</li><li>Reverse Osmosis</li><li>Nanofiltration</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The seawater and brackish water desalination market includes various technologies to convert saline water into fresh water. Multistage Flash Distillation (MSF) uses multiple stages of flashing water to vaporize and condense fresh water. Multi-Effect Distillation (MED) operates on similar principles but uses heat recovery across effects. Vapor Compression employs mechanical or thermal methods to compress vapor for condensation. Reverse Osmosis involves pushing water through semipermeable membranes to remove salts. Nanofiltration uses slightly larger pores to retain divalent ions while allowing monovalent ions to pass through.</p></p>
