<p><ul><li>Ground-based Broadband</li><li>Satellite Broadband</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In-flight internet can be categorized into two primary market types: ground-based broadband and satellite broadband. Ground-based broadband relies on terrestrial cellular networks, using signals from ground stations to provide connectivity, offering lower latency and higher speeds. In contrast, satellite broadband utilizes satellites to deliver internet service, enabling coverage in remote areas where ground infrastructure is lacking. While ground-based systems may offer better performance, satellite systems can cater to a wider range of aircraft and flight paths.</p></p>
